Cutting things in your work isn't always great, while it can usher in less procrastination and get your over a hurdle you have been stuck on for awhile it can also have negatives.

So, in light of a recent cut made to my current game I wrote this entry. Check it out.
Preview screenie.
I've been working on a radio feature which is supposed to be a more robust version of an old idea. Basically a souped up version of the external Radio app I would have along the side of my games.

When developing this there were 5 goals in mind:
  • -Mute Function
  • -Good Menu
  • -Fix Overlapping Song Bug
  • -Make Song Selection More Intuitive
  • -Autoplay Random Songs

From those goals we have only met 4 of them, why you ask? I'll explain.
Cutting, it is a nightmare for me as it means I have to stray from my vision and abandon my goal.

Other people are used to it y'know? Cutting things from their projects because it wasn't working or there were issues is just common practice for others. But I make a thing of it to try to avoid straying from my original goals, because I pride myself on my my outlandish ideas and odd design to create fun expierences. After all; my latest project, Sockman, and Many Mans Against You is nothing but odd ideas working together despite their difficulties to create fun expierences.

But this time, I've cut a feature that would turn this Radio from good to great.
Autoplay... It is insanely useful and a flagship feature in things like Spotify and even Youtube Playlists. And for me, someone who has conquered loads of developement hurdles can't fucking beat this is beyond stupid.
I'm dissapointed yeah, but in reality this is just to keep me from delaying this game and allow me to release before the end of summer.

Thats it, I just wanted to log some frustrations I've been having with my game and hopefully give some insight into my project which has taken pretty much all summer.
Release date: sometime next week?
