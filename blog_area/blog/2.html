I'm working on a game, the game is "Grand Grandman's Grand Adventure" and I am having the worst fucking time.

The reason? Cutscenes.
my feelings on cutscenes
I fucking hate this gay shit lemme tell ya, whenever I want to create a cutscene there are 3 options: 1. Actual Cutscene 2. Slideshow 3. Do Something Else

So while creating cutscenes I have lots of problems creating actual cutscenes, and I mean who can blame me? Cutscenes are hard. But I'm no Hunt Down The Freeman, I can't just bake video files in and call it a day. So I've resorted to option 2.

So now I'm faced with another predicament, creating the art for this god-forsaken cutscene. Which not only has to be very well made since the images are lingered on for a bit, but they also have to be made in my "demanding" paint.net style.

It takes forever, so in most cases I end up skimmnig out on the work. Which leads to sloppier cutscenes I'm not happy with!
So, how am I gonna get around this? Because a cutscene has to be made.

The answer? Console Dialogues.
The choice is both a style choice and a time saver, it will most likely be used in other games as it allows me to cut out uneccesary cutscene watching out of games and allow for the player to skip shit if they so desire. I don't have any pictures or resources now on what it should look like. But rest assured that it'll be a game changer for my cutscenes and make them 1000% better all around the board.

The game should release near the end of summer, hopefully before it ends though.
